Characters from Jesus' Time

Jesus Christ

The Son of God, the central figure of Christianity, whose life, death, and resurrection are the foundation of Christian faith.

Scripture References:

  • Birth: Matthew 1:18-25, Luke 2:1-20
  • Baptism: Matthew 3:13-17, Mark 1:9-11, Luke 3:21-22
  • Ministry Begins: Matthew 4:17, Mark 1:14-15
  • Sermon on the Mount: Matthew 5-7
  • Transfiguration: Matthew 17:1-13, Mark 9:2-13, Luke 9:28-36
  • Last Supper: Matthew 26:17-30, Mark 14:12-26, Luke 22:7-23
  • Crucifixion: Matthew 27:32-56, Mark 15:21-41, Luke 23:26-49, John 19:17-37
  • Resurrection: Matthew 28:1-20, Mark 16:1-8, Luke 24:1-53, John 20:1-31

Political Leaders

King Herod the Great

The Roman-appointed king of Judea who ordered the killing of male infants in Bethlehem.

Scripture References:

  • Meeting with Magi: Matthew 2:1-8
  • Killing of Infants: Matthew 2:16-18
  • Death: Matthew 2:19

Herod Antipas

Son of Herod the Great, ruler of Galilee during Jesus's ministry. He ordered John the Baptist's execution and met Jesus during his trial.

Scripture References:

  • Marriage to Herodias: Matthew 14:3-4, Mark 6:17-18
  • Execution of John the Baptist: Matthew 14:1-12, Mark 6:14-29
  • Meeting with Jesus: Luke 23:6-12

Pontius Pilate

The Roman governor who presided over Jesus's trial and ordered his crucifixion.

Scripture References:

  • Trial of Jesus: Matthew 27:11-26, Mark 15:1-15, Luke 23:1-25, John 18:28-19:16
  • Jesus's Death: Matthew 27:57-66, Mark 15:42-47, Luke 23:50-56, John 19:38-42

The Twelve Disciples

Simon Peter

A fisherman who became one of Jesus's closest disciples.

Scripture References:

  • Call to Discipleship: Matthew 4:18-20, Mark 1:16-18, Luke 5:1-11
  • Walking on Water: Matthew 14:28-31
  • Confession of Christ: Matthew 16:13-20
  • Transfiguration: Matthew 17:1-13
  • Denial of Jesus: Matthew 26:69-75
  • Restoration: John 21:15-19

Andrew

Peter's brother, also a fisherman, who was one of the first to follow Jesus.

Scripture References:

  • Call to Discipleship: Matthew 4:18-20, Mark 1:16-18
  • Introducing Peter to Jesus: John 1:40-42
  • Feeding the 5,000: John 6:8-9

James (son of Zebedee)

One of the "Sons of Thunder," part of Jesus's inner circle along with Peter and John.

Scripture References:

  • Call to Discipleship: Matthew 4:21-22, Mark 1:19-20
  • Request for Seats: Matthew 20:20-23, Mark 10:35-40
  • Transfiguration: Matthew 17:1-13, Mark 9:2-13, Luke 9:28-36

John

The "beloved disciple," brother of James and author of the Gospel of John.

Scripture References:

  • Call to Discipleship: Matthew 4:21-22
  • Transfiguration: Mark 9:2-13
  • At the Last Supper: John 13:23-25
  • At the Cross: John 19:26-27
  • At the Empty Tomb: John 20:1-10

Philip

From Bethsaida, he brought Nathanael to Jesus and showed practical concern during the feeding of the 5,000.

Scripture References:

  • Call to Discipleship: John 1:43-51
  • Feeding the 5,000: John 6:5-7
  • Request from Greeks: John 12:20-22

Bartholomew (Nathanael)

The one whom Jesus said had no deceit. He was brought to Jesus by Philip.

Scripture References:

  • Call to Discipleship: John 1:43-51
  • Jesus's Praise: John 1:47-51

Matthew (Levi)

A former tax collector who wrote the Gospel of Matthew. He left his profitable career to follow Jesus.

Scripture References:

  • Call to Discipleship: Matthew 9:9-13, Mark 2:13-14, Luke 5:27-28
  • Feast for Jesus: Matthew 9:10-13, Mark 2:15-17, Luke 5:29-32

Thomas

Called "Didymus" (the twin), known for doubting Jesus's resurrection until seeing him personally.

Scripture References:

  • Willing to Die with Jesus: John 11:16
  • Doubting the Resurrection: John 20:24-25
  • Seeing the Risen Jesus: John 20:26-29

James (son of Alphaeus)

Also known as James the Less, one of the twelve disciples.

Scripture References:

  • Call to Discipleship: Matthew 10:3, Mark 3:18, Luke 6:15, Acts 1:13

Thaddaeus (Judas son of James)

Also called Lebbaeus, one of the twelve disciples.

Scripture References:

  • Call to Discipleship: Matthew 10:3, Mark 3:18, Luke 6:15, Acts 1:13

Simon the Zealot

A member of the zealous nationalist party before following Jesus.

Scripture References:

  • Call to Discipleship: Matthew 10:4, Mark 3:18, Luke 6:15, Acts 1:13

Judas Iscariot

The disciple who betrayed Jesus for thirty pieces of silver.

Scripture References:

  • Call to Discipleship: Matthew 10:4, Mark 3:19, Luke 6:15, Acts 1:13
  • Betrayal of Jesus: Matthew 26:14-16, Mark 14:10-11, Luke 22:3-6
  • Death: Matthew 27:3-5, Acts 1:18-19

Religious Leaders

Caiaphas

The high priest who played a key role in the plot to kill Jesus.

Scripture References:

  • Plot Against Jesus: John 11:49-53
  • Jesus's Trial: Matthew 26:57-68, Mark 14:53-65
  • Questioning Peter: John 18:24

Annas

Former high priest and father-in-law of Caiaphas, who first questioned Jesus after his arrest.

Scripture References:

  • Questioning Jesus: John 18:13-24

Pharisees

Nicodemus

A Pharisee and member of the Sanhedrin who came to Jesus at night and later helped with his burial.

Scripture References:

  • Night Visit to Jesus: John 3:1-21
  • Defending Jesus: John 7:50-52
  • Burial of Jesus: John 19:39-42

Gamaliel

A respected Pharisee and teacher of the Law who advised the Sanhedrin regarding the early Christian movement.

Scripture References:

  • Counsel to the Sanhedrin: Acts 5:34-40
  • Paul's Teacher: Acts 22:3

Simon the Pharisee

A Pharisee who invited Jesus to dine at his house, where a sinful woman anointed Jesus's feet.

Scripture References:

  • Dinner with Jesus: Luke 7:36-50

Sadducees

Caiaphas

The high priest and a Sadducee who played a key role in the plot to kill Jesus.

Scripture References:

  • Plot Against Jesus: John 11:49-53
  • Jesus's Trial: Matthew 26:57-68, Mark 14:53-65
  • Questioning Peter: John 18:24

Annas

Former high priest, a Sadducee, and father-in-law of Caiaphas, who first questioned Jesus after his arrest.

Scripture References:

  • Questioning Jesus: John 18:13-24

John and Alexander

Members of the high-priestly family who were present at Peter and John's trial.

Scripture References:

  • Trial of Peter and John: Acts 4:6

Other Notable Figures

Mary (Mother of Jesus)

The virgin mother of Jesus, chosen by God. She demonstrated great faith and remained devoted to Jesus throughout his ministry.

Scripture References:

  • Annunciation: Luke 1:26-38
  • Visit to Elizabeth: Luke 1:39-56
  • Birth of Jesus: Luke 2:1-20
  • Presentation at Temple: Luke 2:22-38
  • Flight to Egypt: Matthew 2:13-15
  • At Wedding in Cana: John 2:1-11
  • At the Cross: John 19:25-27
  • In the Upper Room: Acts 1:14

Joseph

The earthly father of Jesus, a carpenter from Nazareth who was chosen to raise Jesus.

Scripture References:

  • Betrothal and Dream: Matthew 1:18-25
  • Journey to Bethlehem: Luke 2:1-5
  • Flight to Egypt: Matthew 2:13-15
  • Return to Nazareth: Matthew 2:19-23
  • Finding Jesus in Temple: Luke 2:41-52

John the Baptist

The prophet who prepared the way for Jesus's ministry and baptized him in the Jordan River.

Scripture References:

  • Birth: Luke 1:5-25, 57-80
  • Ministry: Matthew 3:1-12, Mark 1:1-8, Luke 3:1-20
  • Baptism of Jesus: Matthew 3:13-17, Mark 1:9-11, Luke 3:21-22
  • Imprisonment and Death: Matthew 14:1-12, Mark 6:14-29, Luke 3:19-20

Mary Magdalene

A devoted follower of Jesus who was delivered from seven demons.

Scripture References:

  • Deliverance: Luke 8:2
  • At the Cross: Matthew 27:55-56, Mark 15:40-41, John 19:25
  • At the Burial: Matthew 27:61, Mark 15:47
  • At the Resurrection: Matthew 28:1-10, Mark 16:1-8, John 20:1-18

Martha and Mary

Sisters of Lazarus from Bethany who were close friends of Jesus.

Scripture References:

  • Jesus's Visit: Luke 10:38-42
  • Lazarus's Death and Resurrection: John 11:1-44
  • Dinner for Jesus: John 12:1-11

Lazarus

Brother of Martha and Mary whom Jesus raised from the dead.

Scripture References:

  • Death and Resurrection: John 11:1-44
  • Dinner at Bethany: John 12:1-11
  • Plot to Kill Him: John 12:9-11

Zacchaeus

A chief tax collector who climbed a sycamore tree to see Jesus and later repented.

Scripture References:

  • Meeting Jesus: Luke 19:1-10

Barabbas

The prisoner whom the crowd chose to release instead of Jesus.

Scripture References:

  • Release: Matthew 27:15-26, Mark 15:6-15, Luke 23:18-25, John 18:39-40

Simon of Cyrene

The man compelled to carry Jesus's cross to Golgotha.

Scripture References:

  • Carrying the Cross: Matthew 27:32, Mark 15:21, Luke 23:26

Salome

The mother of James and John, who was present at Jesus's crucifixion and resurrection.

Scripture References:

  • Request for Seats: Matthew 20:20-23, Mark 10:35-40
  • At the Cross: Mark 15:40
  • At the Empty Tomb: Mark 16:1-8

Joanna

Wife of Chuza (Herod's steward), who supported Jesus's ministry and witnessed the resurrection.

Scripture References:

  • Supporting Jesus's Ministry: Luke 8:3
  • At the Cross: Luke 23:49
  • At the Empty Tomb: Luke 24:10

Bartimaeus

The blind beggar who called out to Jesus and was healed near Jericho.

Scripture References:

  • Healing: Mark 10:46-52, Luke 18:35-43

Jairus

A synagogue leader whose daughter was raised from the dead by Jesus.

Scripture References:

  • Daughter's Healing: Matthew 9:18-26, Mark 5:21-43, Luke 8:40-56

The Woman with the Issue of Blood

A woman who was healed by touching Jesus' garment after suffering for 12 years.

Scripture References:

  • Healing: Matthew 9:20-22, Mark 5:25-34, Luke 8:43-48

The Centurion

A Roman officer whose great faith impressed Jesus, leading to his servant's healing.

Scripture References:

  • Servant's Healing: Matthew 8:5-13, Luke 7:1-10

Zacchaeus

A wealthy tax collector who climbed a sycamore tree to see Jesus and later repented.

Scripture References:

  • Meeting Jesus: Luke 19:1-10

Blind Bartimaeus

A blind beggar who persistently called out to Jesus and received his sight.

Scripture References:

  • Healing: Mark 10:46-52, Luke 18:35-43

The Woman at the Well

A Samaritan woman who encountered Jesus at Jacob's well and brought her town to meet him.

Scripture References:

  • Encounter with Jesus: John 4:1-42

Mary of Bethany

Sister of Martha and Lazarus who anointed Jesus' feet and chose to sit at his feet and learn.

Scripture References:

  • Learning from Jesus: Luke 10:38-42
  • Anointing Jesus: John 12:1-8
  • Lazarus' Death: John 11:1-44

Joseph of Arimathea

A wealthy member of the Sanhedrin who provided his tomb for Jesus' burial.

Scripture References:

  • Burial of Jesus: Matthew 27:57-60, Mark 15:42-46, Luke 23:50-53, John 19:38-42

Salome

Mother of James and John, who requested special positions for her sons in Jesus' kingdom.

Scripture References:

  • Request for Sons: Matthew 20:20-28
  • At the Cross: Mark 15:40
  • At the Tomb: Mark 16:1

Joanna

Wife of Chuza (Herod's steward) who supported Jesus' ministry and witnessed the resurrection.

Scripture References:

  • Supporting Jesus: Luke 8:1-3
  • At the Tomb: Luke 24:10

Susanna

One of the women who supported Jesus' ministry with their own means.

Scripture References:

  • Supporting Jesus: Luke 8:1-3

Malchus

The high priest's servant whose ear was cut off by Peter and healed by Jesus.

Scripture References:

  • Ear Cut Off: John 18:10
  • Healing: Luke 22:51

Simon of Cyrene

The man who was compelled to carry Jesus' cross to Golgotha.

Scripture References:

  • Carrying the Cross: Matthew 27:32, Mark 15:21, Luke 23:26

The Penitent Thief

One of the criminals crucified with Jesus who repented and was promised paradise.

Scripture References:

  • Conversation with Jesus: Luke 23:39-43

The Syrophoenician Woman

A Gentile woman whose great faith and persistence led to her daughter's healing.

Scripture References:

  • Daughter's Healing: Matthew 15:21-28, Mark 7:24-30

Cleopas

One of the disciples who encountered the risen Jesus on the road to Emmaus.

Scripture References:

  • Road to Emmaus: Luke 24:13-35
