New Testament Characters

Early Church Leaders and Apostles

Paul (Saul of Tarsus)

Former persecutor of Christians who became one of the most influential apostles after his dramatic conversion.

Scripture References:

  • Conversion: Acts 9:1-31
  • Ministry: Acts 13-28
  • Letters: Romans through Philemon

Peter (Simon)

Leader of the early church in Jerusalem and one of Jesus' closest disciples.

Scripture References:

  • Leadership: Acts 1-12
  • Letters: 1 & 2 Peter

James (Brother of Jesus)

Leader of the Jerusalem church and author of the Epistle of James.

Scripture References:

  • Leadership: Acts 15:13-21
  • Letter: Book of James
  • Mentioned: Galatians 1:19

Barnabas

Early church leader who mentored Paul and participated in the first missionary journey.

Scripture References:

  • Introduction: Acts 4:36-37
  • Ministry with Paul: Acts 13-15

Stephen

The first Christian martyr, known for his powerful testimony before the Sanhedrin.

Scripture References:

  • Ministry and Martyrdom: Acts 6-7

Philip the Evangelist

One of the seven deacons who spread the gospel to Samaria and baptized the Ethiopian eunuch.

Scripture References:

  • Ministry: Acts 8:4-40
  • Later Life: Acts 21:8-9

Paul's Associates and Co-workers

Timothy

Paul's trusted protégé and leader of the Ephesian church.

Scripture References:

  • Introduction: Acts 16:1-3
  • Ministry: 1 & 2 Timothy

Titus

Greek Christian who became a trusted church leader and recipient of Paul's letter.

Scripture References:

  • Ministry: 2 Corinthians 7-8
  • Letter: Book of Titus

Silas (Silvanus)

Prophet and missionary who accompanied Paul on his second missionary journey.

Scripture References:

  • Ministry: Acts 15:22-18:5
  • Letters: 1 & 2 Thessalonians

Luke

Physician and historian who wrote the Gospel of Luke and Acts.

Scripture References:

  • Mentioned: Colossians 4:14
  • Writings: Luke 1:1-4, Acts 1:1-3

Mark (John Mark)

Author of the Gospel of Mark and cousin of Barnabas.

Scripture References:

  • Ministry: Acts 12:12, 25
  • Mentioned: Colossians 4:10

Apollos

Eloquent preacher from Alexandria who taught accurately about Jesus.

Scripture References:

  • Ministry: Acts 18:24-28
  • Mentioned: 1 Corinthians 3:4-6

Notable Women in the Early Church

Priscilla

With her husband Aquila, she taught Apollos and supported Paul's ministry.

Scripture References:

  • Ministry: Acts 18:2-3, 18-26
  • Mentioned: Romans 16:3-4

Lydia

Successful businesswoman and first European convert to Christianity.

Scripture References:

  • Conversion: Acts 16:14-15
  • Hospitality: Acts 16:40

Phoebe

Deaconess of the church at Cenchrea who delivered Paul's letter to the Romans.

Scripture References:

  • Ministry: Romans 16:1-2

Dorcas (Tabitha)

Disciple known for her good works and charity, raised from the dead by Peter.

Scripture References:

  • Ministry and Miracle: Acts 9:36-43

Roman Officials and Authorities

Felix

Roman governor who held Paul in custody for two years in Caesarea.

Scripture References:

  • Paul's Trial: Acts 23:23-24:27

Festus

Successor to Felix who heard Paul's case and sent him to Rome.

Scripture References:

  • Paul's Trial: Acts 25:1-26:32

Gallio

Proconsul of Achaia who dismissed charges against Paul in Corinth.

Scripture References:

  • Paul's Trial: Acts 18:12-17

Sergius Paulus

Proconsul of Cyprus who believed after witnessing Paul's confrontation with Elymas.

Scripture References:

  • Conversion: Acts 13:6-12

Jewish Religious Leaders and Opposition

Ananias (High Priest)

High priest who ordered Paul to be struck during his trial.

Scripture References:

  • Paul's Trial: Acts 23:1-5

Gamaliel

Respected Pharisee who advised moderation in dealing with the apostles.

Scripture References:

  • Counsel: Acts 5:34-40
  • Paul's Teacher: Acts 22:3

Alexander the Metalworker

Opponent of Paul who caused him harm in Ephesus.

Scripture References:

  • Opposition: 2 Timothy 4:14-15

Other Significant Figures

Ethiopian Eunuch

Court official converted and baptized by Philip on the Gaza road.

Scripture References:

  • Conversion: Acts 8:26-40

Cornelius

Roman centurion whose conversion marked the gospel's spread to Gentiles.

Scripture References:

  • Conversion: Acts 10:1-48

Agabus

Prophet who predicted a famine and Paul's arrest in Jerusalem.

Scripture References:

  • Prophecies: Acts 11:27-30, 21:10-11

Onesimus

Runaway slave who became a Christian through Paul's ministry.

Scripture References:

  • Story: Book of Philemon
  • Mentioned: Colossians 4:9

Philemon

Christian slave owner to whom Paul wrote regarding Onesimus.

Scripture References:

  • Letter: Book of Philemon

Elymas (Bar-Jesus)

Sorcerer who opposed Paul and was struck blind.

Scripture References:

  • Confrontation: Acts 13:6-12

Eutychus

Young man who fell from a window during Paul's long sermon and was restored to life.

Scripture References:

  • Miracle: Acts 20:7-12

Family Members and Additional Women

Eunice

Timothy's mother, known for her sincere faith which she passed on to her son.

Scripture References:

  • Mentioned: 2 Timothy 1:5
  • Background: Acts 16:1

Lois

Timothy's grandmother who helped nurture his faith from childhood.

Scripture References:

  • Faith: 2 Timothy 1:5

Mary (mother of John Mark)

Owner of the house where believers gathered to pray for Peter's release.

Scripture References:

  • House Church: Acts 12:12

Sapphira

Wife of Ananias who participated in deceiving the early church.

Scripture References:

  • Story: Acts 5:1-11

Rhoda

Servant girl who announced Peter's release from prison.

Scripture References:

  • Story: Acts 12:13-15

Ananias (with Sapphira)

Early church member who lied about his donation and faced divine judgment.

Scripture References:

  • Story: Acts 5:1-11

Aquila

Tentmaker who, with his wife Priscilla, supported Paul's ministry.

Scripture References:

  • Ministry: Acts 18:2-3, 18-26
  • Mentioned: Romans 16:3-4

Crispus

Synagogue ruler in Corinth who believed in the Lord with his household.

Scripture References:

  • Conversion: Acts 18:8
  • Baptism: 1 Corinthians 1:14

Sosthenes

Synagogue leader who was beaten in Corinth and later became a Christian.

Scripture References:

  • In Corinth: Acts 18:17
  • With Paul: 1 Corinthians 1:1
